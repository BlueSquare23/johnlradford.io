### Literacy

To look at pure country ranking is misleading because literacy is a spectrum.
Its not a binary thing and is more complicated than simply can or cannot read. 

Also lots of countries lie about their overall literacy rates. On paper the US
has a 99% literacy rate, however that's misleading at best and an outright lie
at worse.

What's more important is the level people read at. This is tracked using a
standardized assessment developed by the _"Program for the International
Assessment of Adult Competencies (PIAAC)"_.

### Cost of Living

Cost of living is ranked by something called the "Cost of Living Index."

> What Is a Cost of Living Index? 
>
> Cost of living data includes the expenses incurred for food, shelter,
> transportation, energy, clothing, education, healthcare, childcare, and
> entertainment.
>
> A cost of living index tracks how much basic expenses for these categories
> rise over time and in different regions. It can demonstrate how need-based
> expenses vary from one area to another. 

- [Cost of Living Index - investopedia.com](https://www.investopedia.com/ask/answers/100214/how-cost-living-index-calculated.asp)

What matters is a lower Cost of Living Index (COLI) number is better.

![US Cost of Living Map - numbeo.com](/static/img/us-cost-of-living.png)

- [Cost of Living Index by Country 2025 Mid-Year - numbeo.com](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/rankings_by_country.jsp)

Now we have to keep in mind that the US is a developed nation and generally
speaking, developed nations have a higher cost of living.

However, surprise surprise, the US still ranks worse than Germany, France,
Japan, UK, Canada, Australia, etc.
